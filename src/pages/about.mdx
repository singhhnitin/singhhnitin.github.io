# About Me

I'm Nitin Singh, a B.Tech CSE undergraduate student at KIIT University, Bhubaneswar.

I got into coding the way most people from small towns do — out of curiosity. I used to explore different programming languages with a friend, and somewhere along the way it hit me that literally everything around us is built by someone writing code. That feeling never really left.

I first heard about Google Summer of Code back in 2020 when I was in 10th grade. It stuck in my head for years. When the time finally came to apply, I looked at a lot of organisations but DBpedia felt right — it was something I could actually connect with. Hindi is my first language, and the gap between what Hindi Wikipedia contains and what the Hindi DBpedia chapter actually structures is striking once you see it. Most people in our country who go into NLP end up working on English or other foreign languages because that's where the resources and benchmarks are. Hindi, despite being spoken by over 600 million people, is still largely untapped in this space. That bothered me enough to want to do something about it.

This is my first GSoC. I'm working on fine-tuning small language models for Hindi relational triple extraction and building a human-in-the-loop feedback interface that turns reviewer corrections into training data. It's harder than I expected and more interesting than I hoped.
